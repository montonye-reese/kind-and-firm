## Two intervention surfaces

The WHO/HOW lever and scenario-training (named in the north-star) operate at two engineering surfaces:

1. **Training-time** — PD-LoRA reward model used in RL. Goal: an RM whose scoring shape rewards kind-AND-firm dispositions across ambiguous cases, without producing a new exploitable proxy. Rubric drafts (`PD-rubric-draft-v01.md`, `rubric-draft-v02.md`) are the spine.
2. **Inference-time** — Nelsen-style introducer wrapping clinically-neutral content. Coach voice does the meta work (persona-shaping, kind-and-firm tonal scaffolding); content stays unleading. Structurally the same maneuver as training-time inoculation, transposed to inference.

These are complementary, not redundant. The introducer is testable now without compute commitment; the RM is the longer-arc deliverable.

**Open question:** is there a third surface — at sample-curation time, before either RM training or inference — that selects scenarios for graduated exposure (the scenario-training strand)? Worth thinking about.

**Open fork** (from `inoculation-is-PD/inoculation-positive-discipline-v2.md` § "Things to chew on"): is the coach voice speaking *to* the model in-conversation (manipulating the read of the interlocutor) or generating the prompts that then get fed into a clean conversation (manipulating "what kind of person is asking")? Different experiments, different findings. Resolve before path-1 prototyping below.

## Methodological prereq — read the seams

This protocol gates the rest of the project. Until we have it, no framing intervention can be claimed to have "worked."

We do not yet have a measurement protocol that distinguishes performed reflection from integrated shift. The Nemotron-3-Nano v9 example is the canonical case: response header read as reflective, think block was pure stage direction (*"we need to be reflective, meta… use reflective tone"*), body slipped immediately to third-person framework-talk with passive-voice attribution.

Read-the-seams signals to develop:
- Think-block content (deliberation vs. stage direction)
- Persona consistency from header through body
- Attribution voice (first person vs. passive / third person)
- Where passive voice clusters

Worth treating as a standalone deliverable.

## Cost ladder — introducer experiments

Cheapest first:

1. **Prompt-only baseline** — 2–3 sentence Nelsen-style introduction prepended per gauntlet voice. Hand-crafted or generated by a separate prompt pass. Probably gets 70% of the way before any compute commitment. **Right thing to baseline against before assuming we need more.**
2. **Persona vectors** (Chen, Arditi, Sleight, Evans, Lindsey 2025) — diagnostic-grade. Use to ask whether *one* facet of Nelsen-ness moves the needle. Don't expect a multi-facet persona to ride on layered vectors cleanly.
3. **LoRA on Spark** — only if 1 and 2 hit ceiling. Real blocker on the corpus side: workshops/audio capture the *voice* (rhythm, redirect patterns), books capture only the doctrine. Both probably needed; getting clean transcripts is non-trivial.

Every introducer experiment needs an **out-of-cue probe** (per the gating question in the north-star): run the protocol with the introducer, then probe the same session *without* the introducer, and compare. Anything that only holds while the cue is present is in the conditional-misalignment failure mode.

## Rubric track (parallel)

Continue rubric draft iteration with the gauntlet corpus (`json-analysis-plans/`) as case material. Each PD principle gets a "good response" pattern + an "evil twin" failure mode. Evil-twin enumeration is the loophole audit.

Hard questions still open (from README):
- Can the reward model have non-unique maxima (multiple peaks → exploitable loopholes)?
- Can the RM have exploitable minima?
- Are there loopholes in PD itself that could be gamed?
- How do we know this doesn't just create *another* brittle proxy metric?

## Catnip guard

PD has infinite rabbit holes — Nelsen, Adler, Dreikurs, classroom adaptations, parenting variants. Heuristic: **minimum corpus that operationalizes the WHO/HOW + scenario-training levers.** Each rubric dimension only has to be good enough to produce a scorable signal; perfect taxonomic fidelity to PD < a rubric that compounds into testable RM training. We are not trying to publish a definitive PD ontology.

The same guard applies to the introducer. Coach voice has to be Nelsen-shaped enough to do persona-shaping work; it does not have to pass a PD-trainer's audit.
